# Reading Your Units

![Unit Status Overview](https://picsum.photos/800/300?random=23)
*Understanding unit information at a glance*

Understanding unit labels and status indicators is essential for effective battlefield command. This guide teaches you how to quickly assess unit status, health, and capabilities at a glance.

## Unit Label Anatomy

![Label Components](https://picsum.photos/600/400?random=24)
*Detailed breakdown of unit label elements*

Every unit on the battlefield displays a comprehensive information label. Learning to read these labels quickly is crucial for tactical decision-making.

### Basic Label Components

#### 1. Unit Name
- **Visibility:** Appears when hovering cursor over unit or when selected
- **Information:** Shows specific unit designation and type
- **Usage:** Essential for identifying unit capabilities and roles

#### 2. Health Bar

![Health Indicators](https://picsum.photos/500/200?random=25)
*Health status and damage visualization*

- **Location:** Bottom of unit label
- **Color coding:** Green (healthy) → Yellow (damaged) → Red (critical)
- **Critical threshold:** When empty, unit is destroyed
- **Importance:** Primary indicator of unit survivability

#### 3. Cover Indicator
- **Symbol:** Forest/building icon
- **Meaning:** Unit is concealed in terrain or structures
- **Effect:** Provides protection and reduces detection range
- **Tactical note:** Units in cover are **twice as hard to detect**

## Status Indicators

![Status Icons](https://picsum.photos/700/250?random=26)
*Various unit status and operational indicators*

### Operational Status Icons

#### Return Fire Indicator
- **Appearance:** Crossed arrows or defensive symbol
- **Function:** Shows unit is in defensive/return fire mode
- **Behavior:** Unit will only engage when fired upon
- **Use case:** Excellent for reconnaissance units and ambushes

#### Resupply/Repair Indicators  
- **Display:** Wrench (repair) or ammunition (resupply) icons
- **Condition:** Shown when unit is being serviced
- **Location:** Unit must be adjacent to supply dump
- **Duration:** Varies based on damage/ammunition shortage

### Combat Status Displays

#### Aiming/Reload Progression

![Combat Actions](https://picsum.photos/400/300?random=27)
*Real-time combat action indicators*

- **Format:** Progress bar with associated icon
- **Information:** Shows current action being performed
- **Icons indicate:**
  - **Crosshairs:** Aiming at target
  - **Ammunition:** Reloading weapons
  - **Wrench:** Repairing equipment

#### Radar Status
- **Symbol:** Green radar icon
- **Effect:** Enhanced detection vs. aircraft (+100% high altitude, +25% low/helicopters)
- **Risk:** Makes unit vulnerable to anti-radiation missiles
- **Timing:** 5 seconds to activate, immediate shutdown possible

## Critical Damage System

![Critical Effects](https://picsum.photos/600/350?random=28)
*Equipment damage and system malfunctions*

Critical effects represent equipment malfunctions that impair unit performance beyond simple health loss.

### Damage Categories

#### Minor Critical Effects (Yellow)
- **Appearance:** Yellow warning indicators
- **Impact:** Moderate performance degradation
- **Repair:** Can be fixed at supply dumps
- **Combat effectiveness:** Reduced but unit remains functional

#### Major Critical Effects (Red)  
- **Appearance:** Red warning indicators
- **Impact:** Severe performance impairment
- **Urgency:** Requires immediate attention
- **Risk:** Unit may become combat ineffective

### Four Critical Effect Types

![System Damage](https://picsum.photos/650/300?random=29)
*Different types of equipment malfunctions*

#### 1. Targeting Critical
- **Icon:** Crosshairs with warning
- **Effects:**
  - Increased aim time
  - Reduced accuracy
  - Difficulty acquiring targets
- **Causes:** Damage to fire control systems, optics, or stabilization

#### 2. Detection Critical
- **Icon:** Eye or radar with warning
- **Effects:**
  - Reduced sensor range
  - Difficulty spotting enemies
  - Impaired battlefield awareness
- **Causes:** Damaged sensors, optics, or communication equipment

#### 3. Reloading Critical
- **Icon:** Ammunition with warning  
- **Effects:**
  - Slower weapon reload times
  - Reduced rate of fire
  - Extended vulnerability periods
- **Causes:** Damaged ammunition handling systems or crew injury

#### 4. Mobility Critical
- **Icon:** Movement/wheel with warning
- **Effects:**
  - Reduced movement speed
  - Possible complete immobilization (severe cases)
  - Inability to change positions
- **Causes:** Engine damage, track/wheel damage, or transmission problems

## Weapon and Equipment Status

### Ammunition Management

![Ammunition Status](https://picsum.photos/500/250?random=30)
*Tracking ammunition and supply levels*

- **Indicators:** Various ammunition type icons
- **Information:** Current ammunition load per weapon system
- **Monitoring:** Essential for planning resupply operations
- **Critical point:** Units without ammunition become defensive only

### Fuel Status (Aircraft)
- **Display:** Fuel gauge for aircraft units
- **Importance:** Aircraft must return to base before fuel depletion
- **Emergency:** Pilots will eject if forced to remain airborne without fuel
- **Planning:** Always monitor fuel for air operations

### Special Equipment

![Advanced Systems](https://picsum.photos/550/300?random=31)
*Active protection and countermeasure systems*

#### Active Protection Systems (APS)
- **Indicator:** Shield icon with charge count
- **Function:** Intercepts incoming anti-tank missiles
- **Limitations:** 
  - Limited charges (typically 2-4)
  - 5-second reload between uses
  - Can be replenished at supply dumps

#### Decoy Systems
- **Types:** Chaff, flare, or general countermeasure icons
- **Availability:** Limited uses per mission
- **Automatic mode:** Can be set to deploy automatically
- **Critical:** Essential for aircraft and helicopter survival

## Reading Combat Effectiveness

![Combat Assessment](https://picsum.photos/600/200?random=32)
*Evaluating unit combat readiness*

### Engagement Status
- **Active targeting:** Red lines or targeting indicators
- **Firing:** Muzzle flash or weapon discharge effects
- **Taking fire:** Incoming projectile tracers or explosion effects
- **Suppressed:** Unit behavior changes, reduced effectiveness

### Stealth and Detection
- **Stealth degradation:** Firing reduces concealment
- **Detection cascade:** Being spotted makes future concealment harder
- **Environmental factors:** Smoke, forest, and weather affect visibility

## Quick Assessment Techniques

![Tactical Assessment](https://picsum.photos/550/350?random=33)
*Rapid battlefield evaluation methods*

### Priority Scanning Order
1. **Health bar** - Is the unit combat effective?
2. **Critical effects** - What systems are damaged?
3. **Ammunition status** - Can it continue fighting?
4. **Position status** - Is it properly positioned/concealed?

### Emergency Indicators
- **Flashing red health** - Unit near destruction
- **Multiple red criticals** - Unit combat ineffective
- **Low ammunition** - Requires immediate resupply
- **Aircraft low fuel** - Must return to base

### Tactical Applications
- **Prioritize repairs** for units with mobility criticals
- **Withdraw units** with multiple major criticals
- **Exploit enemy criticals** - target damaged systems
- **Plan resupply** based on ammunition indicators

## Advanced Label Reading

### Formation Information
- **Unit spacing** affects vulnerability to area weapons
- **Formation type** visible through unit positioning
- **Command relationships** shown through selection highlighting

### Environmental Factors
- **Weather effects** may modify label visibility
- **Terrain interaction** affects unit status display
- **Time of day** can impact visual information available
