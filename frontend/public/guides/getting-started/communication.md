# Communication 101

Effective communication is the cornerstone of victory in Broken Arrow. With 5 players per team, coordinated action separates winning teams from losing ones. This guide covers all communication tools and best practices.

## Why Communication Matters

> **"Individual performance matters, but communication is key."** - Official Broken Arrow Guide

### The Reality of 5v5 Combat
- **Complex battlefield** with multiple simultaneous engagements
- **Shared objectives** requiring coordinated assaults
- **Limited resources** demanding efficient allocation
- **Time pressure** with 45-minute match limits
- **Fog of war** - no single player sees everything

### Communication Multiplies Effectiveness
- **Intelligence sharing** - spotting reports and threat warnings
- **Resource coordination** - supply sharing and mutual support
- **Tactical synchronization** - coordinated attacks and defenses
- **Adaptability** - rapid response to changing situations

## In-Game Communication Markers

The marker system provides visual coordination tools for teams using different communication methods.

### Four Marker Types

#### Warning Marker (F1)
- **Purpose:** Alert teammates to threats
- **Use cases:**
  - Enemy movement spotted
  - Incoming artillery
  - Ambush positions identified
  - Danger areas to avoid

#### Attack Marker (F2)  
- **Purpose:** Coordinate offensive operations
- **Use cases:**
  - Target selection for focus fire
  - Assault objectives
  - Enemy weakness exploitation
  - Synchronized attacks

#### Defend Marker (F3)
- **Purpose:** Request defensive support
- **Use cases:**
  - Objective under threat
  - Requesting reinforcements
  - Establishing defensive positions
  - Fallback positions

#### Supplies Marker (F4)
- **Purpose:** Mark supply and logistics needs
- **Use cases:**
  - Supply dump locations
  - Resupply requests
  - Safe logistics routes
  - Ammunition sharing points

### Marker Management
- **Maximum 5 markers** active simultaneously
- **1-minute duration** before automatic removal
- **Right-click** to manually remove markers
- **Drag and drop** to reposition existing markers
- **Coordinate with team** - avoid marker spam

## In-Game Text Chat

### Accessing Chat
- **Chat button** in top-right corner of interface
- **Resizable window** - drag bottom-left corner to adjust
- **Team communication** only (no all-chat during beta)

### Chat Etiquette
- **Stay courteous** - toxic behavior results in beta bans
- **Use appropriate language** - maintain professional atmosphere
- **Provide useful information** - avoid chat spam
- **Support teammates** - encouragement improves performance

### Effective Chat Usage
- **Supplement markers** with additional details
- **Share intelligence** that markers cannot convey
- **Coordinate complex plans** requiring explanation
- **Provide encouragement** during difficult moments

## Communication Protocols

### Pre-Match Communication
- **Introduce yourself** and preferred role
- **Discuss battlegroup compositions** and specializations
- **Plan initial deployment** and responsibility areas
- **Establish communication preferences** (Discord vs. in-game)

### Early Game Coordination
- **Share reconnaissance** - enemy positions and compositions
- **Coordinate initial moves** - avoid conflicting deployments
- **Establish supply lines** - agree on logistics responsibilities
- **Identify threats** - priority targets and danger areas

### Mid-Game Communication
- **Tactical updates** - changing battlefield conditions  
- **Resource sharing** - supply distribution and requests
- **Mutual support** - reinforcement calls and responses
- **Objective coordination** - capture timing and assignments

### Late Game Coordination
- **Final pushes** - all-out coordinated assaults
- **Defensive holds** - last-stand positioning
- **Resource management** - ammunition and fuel conservation
- **Victory conditions** - timer management and objective control

## Advanced Communication Techniques

### Callout Systems
- **Use landmarks** - "Enemy armor north of the church"
- **Grid references** - learn map grid systems for precision
- **Relative positions** - "Behind you, 200 meters"
- **Clock positions** - "Contacts at your 3 o'clock"

### Information Hierarchy
1. **Immediate threats** - incoming fire, ambushes
2. **Tactical opportunities** - exposed enemies, weak points
3. **Strategic updates** - objective status, resource needs
4. **General information** - movement updates, reconnaissance

### Listening Skills
- **Acknowledge** important information
- **Ask for clarification** when needed
- **Filter information** - focus on actionable intelligence
- **Maintain awareness** of overall team communication flow

## Communication for New Players

### Start Simple
- **Use markers** - easier than complex voice coordination
- **Listen more than speak** initially
- **Ask questions** - experienced players will help
- **Focus on essential information** - threats and opportunities

### Building Confidence
- **Practice callouts** in low-pressure situations
- **Learn common terminology** through community interaction
- **Find patient teammates** willing to teach
- **Gradually increase communication complexity**

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Information overload** - too much unnecessary detail
- **Poor timing** - interrupting critical communications
- **Unclear callouts** - vague or confusing information
- **Negative attitude** - criticism without constructive solutions

## Team Building Through Communication

### Finding Regular Teammates
- **Discord community** - most reliable source
- **Good match experiences** - add effective communicators
- **Patience building** - developing team chemistry takes time
- **Skill diversity** - teams need various specialization expertise

### Developing Team Synergy
- **Regular practice** - consistent team composition
- **Role specialization** - each player has strengths
- **Communication shortcuts** - develop team-specific callouts
- **Post-match reviews** - discuss what worked and what didn't

Remember: Great communication isn't about talking more—it's about sharing the right information at the right time!
