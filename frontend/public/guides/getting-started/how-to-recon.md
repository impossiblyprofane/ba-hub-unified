## Types of Recon Units

Here I will discuss the basic types of recon units in the reconnaissance tab and my personal rating for each type

**Sniper Squad** – These sneaky bois can be equipped with either suppressed rifles or Anti Material Rifles. And some come with laser pointers for guided munitions. Good for overwatch of the frontline, enemy backline surveillance and flank security. Hard to see by the enemy unless they have their own snipers or if you run them out into the open. Will die to a stiff breeze. Useful in all phases. **Rating: A**

**Combat Recon Infantry**: Infantry with better optics than your regulars in the Infantry tab. Generally better stealth as well, depending on your specialization, they can be equipped with MANPADS, heavy AT or thermobaric. Better survivability than snipers but generally you should not be storming buildings with them. Place these in essential places on the frontline **Rating: B**

**Recon Cars**: They have four wheels, they go fast, can beat anything to where it wants to go on the ground. Some have or can be equipped with machine guns and can harass soft-skinned vehicles. Will die to a pilot if left unattended **Rating: D**

**Armored Recon**: This will include all recon units that are immune to small arms fire.
- **Unarmed**: Think M981 and its Russian counterpart, they have laser pointers and can see stuff but will die quickly with no means of defense. **Rating: F**
- **Armed**: The skirmishers and primary enablers of your army. Place these in forward positions to detect and destroy enemy recon and harass, infantry, transports and helicopters and in the case of the 3K, Bradley, and Recon tanks, armored vehicles. If you want to attack without taking eyewatering casualties take these. If you are feeling **Rating: S**// Bradley and BRM 3k: **S++**

**Drones**: Slow, generally unarmed and sitting ducks. But great vision and can be good to find enemy recon hiding on your flanks or who snuck through a gap. Shoutout to the sentinel which give vision over a massive area. If your enemy doesn't have a competent AA net, congrats you now have maphacks as long as its alive **Rating: D**/ Sentinel: **A**

## I. OPENER: INTELLIGENCE PREPARATION OF THE BATTLEFIELD (IPB)

**Objective:**
Establish initial battlefield awareness, confirm or deny enemy movement and force composition. In military terms, this aligns with IPB and Initial Zone Reconnaissance.

**In-Game Execution:**
- **Deployable Units**: Use fast recon vehicles, combat recon infantry and snipers to get vision over the area your main force is going.
- **Insertion Tactics**:
  - Air recon (e.g. recon infantry in helicopters) for rapid map coverage.
  - Ground recon to secure key choke points, bridges, and cover routes.
- **Initial Focus Areas**:
  - Likely enemy reinforcement routes (MSRs – Main Supply Routes).
  - High ground and intersections for overwatch and spotting.
  - Objectives (airfields, FOB zones, factories).

## II. PHASE ONE: SCREENING AND FORWARD SECURITY

**Objective:**
Provide early warning, delay enemy recon, and maintain situational awareness. Units should remain hidden and avoid decisive engagement.

**In-Game Execution:**
- **Screen with Passive Recon**: Use optics-focused recon units on hold fire to maintain concealment.
- **Create a Recon "Net"**: Space recon units across the front to cover all terrain approaches (urban, forest, open).
- **Bait & Observe**: Send light infantry (e.g., Spetsnaz Recon or Green Berets) to key forests or buildings to bait enemy movement and reveal armor.

**in Play:**
- **Screening**: A light, mobile force operating ahead of the main body to provide early warning.
- **Security Operations**: Prevent enemy recon from determining your main force's disposition.

## III. PHASE TWO: RECONNAISSANCE-IN-FORCE

**Objective:**
Deliberately probe enemy defenses to determine strength, composition, and possible gaps. This is an aggressive escalation from screening.

**In-Game Execution:**
- **Push with Heavier Recon Elements**: Deploy recon vehicles with anti-tank capability (e.g., M3A3 Bradley, 3K, or Recon Tanks).
- **Combined Arms Recon**: Pair recon with fast-response units (tanks or infantry in IFVs) just behind the recon screen.
- **Engage Weak Points**: If enemy lines are thin or disorganized, escalate into a limited objective attack.

**in Play:**
- **Reconnaissance in Force (RIF)**: A deliberate attack to provoke enemy response and reveal positions, using combat-capable units.

## IV. PHASE THREE: BATTLEFIELD DOMINANCE THROUGH RECON STRIKES

**Objective:**
Shape the battlefield in preparation for full-scale offensive. Use recon to direct artillery, airstrikes, and seize strategic terrain with.

**In-Game Execution:**
- **Fire Support Targeting**: Recon identifies and continuously updates positions of high-value targets (SAMs, artillery, HQs) for SEAD/air/artillery.
- **Maintain Flank Recon**: Prevent flanking maneuvers with flanking recon teams or UAVs behind forest lines and bridges.

**in Play:**
- **Economy of Force**: Using limited recon teams to fix enemy forces while main effort develops elsewhere.

## Tips for Success

- **Keep Recon Mobile**: Static recon dies fast. Relocate every 1–2 minutes.
- **Use Terrain**: Forests, ridges, and buildings provide concealment and LOS advantage.
- **Integrate with Support**: A recon unit spotting a tank column is only useful if followed by ATGMs, artillery, or air support.
- **Redundancy Matters**: Don't rely on a single UAV or recon team—layer assets for resilience.

## PRINCIPLES OF RECON
*Army Techniques Publication (APT) 3-21.8*

**Ensure continuous reconnaissance:**
Reconnaissance efforts should be ongoing, not just a one-time operation. This ensures a constant stream of information about the evolving situation.

**Do not keep reconnaissance assets in reserve:**
Reconnaissance assets should be actively engaged in gathering information, rather than being held back for later use. They should be strategically positioned to maximize their effectiveness.

**Orient on the reconnaissance objective:**
All reconnaissance efforts should be focused on the primary goal, which is to obtain specific information about the target area or enemy. This ensures that resources are allocated effectively and that the most relevant data is collected.

**Report information rapidly and accurately:**
Timely and accurate reporting of collected information is crucial for decision-making. This ensures that information is readily available and used effectively.

**Retain freedom of maneuver:**
Reconnaissance assets should be able to move and adjust their position as needed to respond to changing situations. This flexibility allows them to gather information effectively and avoid being pinned down.

**Gain and maintain enemy contact:**
While avoiding unnecessary engagements, reconnaissance efforts should aim to identify and monitor the enemy. This helps to understand their movements, tactics, and strengths.

**Develop the situation rapidly:**
Reconnaissance should continuously assess the situation and adapt to new information. This ensures that the information gathered is relevant and useful for decision-making.
